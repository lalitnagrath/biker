# Tyre Pressure Guide for Indian Motorcycles

Maintaining correct tyre pressure is one of the simplest yet most impactful maintenance tasks you can do. It affects safety, fuel efficiency, tyre life, and ride comfort. Yet most riders check their tyre pressure only when something feels wrong.

## Why Tyre Pressure Matters

### Safety

- **Under-inflated tyres** — Poor handling, increased stopping distance, risk of blowout
- **Over-inflated tyres** — Reduced grip, harsh ride, uneven wear
- **Correct pressure** — Optimal grip, predictable handling, safe braking

### Fuel Efficiency

Properly inflated tyres reduce rolling resistance, which can improve fuel efficiency by 3-5%. For a bike that gives 35 kmpl, that's an extra 1-2 km per liter.

### Tyre Life

- Under-inflation causes edge wear and reduces tyre life by up to 25%
- Over-inflation causes center wear and makes tyres more susceptible to damage
- Correct pressure ensures even wear and maximum lifespan

### Ride Comfort

- Too low — Bouncy, wallowy ride
- Too harsh — Every bump transmitted to handlebars
- Just right — Balanced, controlled, comfortable

## When to Check Tyre Pressure

### Before Every Ride (Quick Visual)

- Look at the tyre — does it look flat?
- Bounce the bike — does it feel soft?

### Weekly (With Gauge)

- Use a reliable digital gauge
- Check when tyres are cold (before riding)
- Adjust as needed

### Before Long Rides (Essential)

- Check and adjust for load
- Consider weather conditions
- Don't forget the spare tyre if carrying one

### After Temperature Changes

- Every 10°C drop in temperature reduces pressure by about 1 PSI
- Winter mornings may require more frequent checks

## How to Check and Adjust Tyre Pressure

### What You Need

- Digital tyre pressure gauge
- Portable air compressor or find a petrol station
- Your bike's recommended pressure values

### Step-by-Step

1. **Check when cold** — Ride less than 1 km or let the bike sit for 3+ hours
2. **Remove valve cap** — Keep it safe (they're easy to lose)
3. **Press gauge onto valve** — Firm, straight press for accurate reading
4. **Compare to recommendation** — Front and rear may differ
5. **Add or release air** — Use compressor to add, press valve core to release
6. **Recheck** — Verify the final pressure
7. **Replace valve cap** — Keeps dirt out

## Effects of Incorrect Pressure

### Under-Inflation (Too Low)

- **Ride feel**: Heavy, sluggish steering
- **Visual**: Tyre sidewall bulges
- **Effects**: Increased fuel consumption, poor handling, uneven edge wear
- **Risk**: Blowout from overheating sidewall

### Over-Inflation (Too High)

- **Ride feel**: Harsh, bouncy, reduced grip
- **Visual**: Tyre looks balloon-like
- **Effects**: Reduced contact patch, center wear, harsh ride
- **Risk**: Reduced grip in wet conditions

### Correct Pressure

- **Ride feel**: Balanced, responsive, comfortable
- **Visual**: Even contact with road
- **Effects**: Optimal fuel efficiency, even wear, maximum grip
- **Result**: Safe, enjoyable riding

## Special Conditions

### Highway Riding

- Add 2-3 PSI for sustained high speeds
- Better stability at 80+ kmph
- Reduced heat buildup

### Off-Road

- Reduce pressure for better grip (18-20 PSI)
- Use with caution — risk of rim damage
- Return to normal pressure for road riding

### Monsoon

- Maintain factory recommended pressure
- Don't reduce for "more grip" — it's a myth
- Check more frequently due to water exposure

### Heavy Load (Pillion + Luggage)

- Increase rear pressure by 4-6 PSI
- Check front pressure with full load
- Recheck after reducing load

## Common Tyre Pressure Myths

### Myth 1: "Lower pressure gives more grip"

**Reality**: While very slight under-inflation can increase the contact patch, it's dangerous. It causes overheating, poor handling, and blowout risk.

### Myth 2: "Higher pressure makes tyres last longer"

**Reality**: Over-inflation causes center wear, reducing overall tyre life and compromising safety.

### Myth 3: "Cold weather means lower pressure"

**Reality**: Cold weather naturally reduces pressure. You should maintain the same pressure by adding air, not reducing it.

### Myth 4: "New tyres need higher pressure"

**Reality**: Follow manufacturer recommendations regardless of tyre age.

## Final Thoughts

Tyre pressure is a simple thing that makes a huge difference. Spend 2 minutes checking it weekly, and you'll enjoy:

- Longer tyre life

- More comfortable rides
