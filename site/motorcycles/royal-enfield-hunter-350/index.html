Complete Accessory Guide for Hunter 350

Everything you need to know before buying accessories for your Hunter 350.

🚙 Helmet

Easy to Buy
📜 Why You Need It

A helmet is legally mandatory in India and the single most important piece of safety gear. For the Hunter 350, we recommend a full-face helmet for maximum protection. A standard full-face helmet offers the best protection-to-value ratio.

What to Look For

  • ✓ ISI certification (mandatory in India)
  • ✓ DOT or ECE certification for international safety
  • ✓ Proper fit — should be snug but not uncomfortable
  • ✓ Good ventilation for Indian climate
  • ✓ Anti-fog visor if you ride in humid areas
  • ✓ Quick-release visor mechanism

Common Mistakes

⚠ Warning
  • Don't buy a helmet just because it looks good — safety first
  • Never use a helmet older than 5 years — materials degrade
  • Don't buy one size larger thinking it'll be "more comfortable"
  • Avoid novelty helmets that don't meet safety standards

Installation Difficulty

No installation needed — just make sure it fits correctly.

Compatibility with Hunter 350

All helmets are universal. However, if you ride with a pillion frequently, consider buying a helmet with Bluetooth connectivity for communication.

Our Recommendation

Editor's Pick: We recommend starting with a mid-range full-face helmet that offers ISI + DOT certification. The extra 2,000-3,000 over a basic helmet is worth it for the added safety features and comfort.

📱 Phone Mount

Easy to Install
📜 Why You Need It

A phone mount is essential for navigation, especially when exploring new routes. Modern phones have excellent cameras and GPS — a good mount lets you use them safely while riding. The Hunter 350's handlebar setup works well with most mount types.

What to Look For

  • ✓ Vibration dampening (protects phone camera OIS)
  • ✓ 360-degree rotation for portrait/landscape
  • ✓ Secure locking mechanism
  • ✓ Weather-resistant materials
  • ✓ Compatible with your phone size (4.7" to 6.7")
  • ✓ Easy one-hand operation

Common Mistakes

⚠ Warning
  • Cheap mounts can vibrate loose — always check tightness before rides
  • Phone vibration can damage camera OIS (optical image stabilization) over time
  • Don't mount where it blocks your view of the road
  • Avoid mounts that require tools to remove your phone

Installation Difficulty

Easy. Most mounts clamp onto handlebars without any drilling. Takes 5-10 minutes.

Compatibility with Hunter 350

The Hunter 350 has standard handlebar diameter (22.2mm / 7/8"). All universal phone mounts will fit. For the cleanest look, consider a handlebar-end mount.

Our Recommendation

Editor's Pick: Always choose a mount with vibration dampening. The ₹500-1000 extra for a quality mount with vibration protection is cheaper than a phone camera repair (₹5,000-8,000).

🛡 Crash Guard

Moderate Install
📜 Why You Need It

A crash guard protects your Hunter 350's engine and bodywork in case of a fall. It's especially important if you're a new rider or ride on roads with unpredictable traffic. A good crash guard can save you thousands in repair costs from just one tip-over.

What to Look For

  • ✓ High-quality steel or aluminium construction
  • ✓ Engine and frame protection coverage
  • ✓ Slider/protector ends for better slide resistance
  • ✓ Proper fitment for Hunter 350
  • ✓ Doesn't interfere with leg positioning
  • ✓ Powder-coated or rust-resistant finish

Common Mistakes

⚠ Warning
  • Generic guards may not fit properly — always check model compatibility
  • Overly bulky guards can make the bike wider and harder to filter in traffic
  • Cheap welds can break on impact — invest in quality
  • Ensure it doesn't block access to the oil filter or spark plug

Installation Difficulty

Moderate. Requires basic tools and 30-45 minutes. May need to remove a few fairing bolts. Consider professional installation if you're not comfortable with basic mechanical work.

Compatibility with Hunter 350

Always buy model-specific crash guards. The Hunter 350 has specific mounting points that ensure proper fitment and protection. Universal guards may leave gaps in coverage.

Our Recommendation

Editor's Pick: For the Hunter 350, a frame-mounted crash guard with slider ends offers the best balance of protection and aesthetics. Budget ₹2,500-4,000 for a quality option.

🎫 Bike Cover

Easy to Use
📜 Why You Need It

India's harsh climate — extreme sun, monsoon rains, and dusty conditions — can damage your Hunter 350's paint and seat. A good bike cover protects against UV damage, water seepage, and bird droppings. It's a small investment that keeps your bike looking new for years.

What to Look For

  • ✓ Waterproof or water-resistant material
  • ✓ UV protection coating
  • ✓ Breathable fabric (prevents moisture buildup)
  • ✓ Secure tie-down straps or elastic hem
  • ✓ Mirror pockets for secure fit
  • ✓ Reflective strips for night visibility

Common Mistakes

⚠ Warning
  • Don't use a plastic tarp — it traps moisture and causes rust
  • Cheap covers fade and tear within months
  • Don't cover a hot bike — let it cool first to prevent moisture buildup
  • Ensure proper fit — too loose flaps in wind, too tight is hard to put on

Installation Difficulty

Trivial. Just drape over the bike and secure. Takes 2 minutes.

Compatibility with Hunter 350

Bike covers are generally universal but check the size chart. The Hunter 350 falls in the standard size range. If you have accessories like a tall windscreen or saddlebags, consider sizing up.

Our Recommendation

Editor's Pick: A waterproof cover with UV protection and breathable fabric is worth the extra ₹300-500 over basic options. Your Hunter 350's paint will thank you.

⚙ Chain Lube

Easy to Apply
📜 Why You Need It

A poorly maintained chain reduces your Hunter 350's performance, fuel efficiency, and can lead to expensive repairs. Regular lubrication keeps the chain running smooth, extends its life by 2-3x, and ensures power is transferred efficiently to the rear wheel.

What to Look For

  • ✓ O-ring or X-ring compatible formula
  • ✓ Water-resistant (essential for monsoon riding)
  • ✓ Doesn't attract excessive dust
  • ✓ Spray can for easy application
  • ✓ Anti-rust properties
  • ✓ Long-lasting formula (reduces application frequency)

Common Mistakes

⚠ Warning
  • Never use WD-40 as chain lube — it's a solvent, not a lubricant
  • Don't over-lubricate — excess lube attracts dirt and grit
  • Apply lube to a clean chain — dirty lube accelerates wear
  • Don't apply right before a ride — let it dry for 10-15 minutes

Application Difficulty

Very easy. Clean chain, spray lube on inner rollers, rotate wheel, let dry. Takes 10-15 minutes. Recommended every 500-700 km.

Compatibility with Hunter 350

The Hunter 350 uses a standard roller chain. Any quality chain lube designed for O-ring or X-ring chains will work. Avoid generic "chain wax" products — they're designed for different chain types.

Our Recommendation

Editor's Pick: A quality chain lube costs ₹300-500 and lasts 3-4 months. That's less than ₹150/month to keep your drivetrain in perfect condition. Always keep a can in your garage.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Royal Enfield Hunter 350 is priced at 1,49,900 (ex-showroom). On-road prices may vary depending on your city and variant.

The Royal Enfield Hunter 350 offers a mileage of approximately 36 kmpl.

The Hunter 350 is primarily designed for city commuting and short weekend rides. However, with proper accessories like a comfortable seat and windscreen, it can handle moderate long-distance touring.
