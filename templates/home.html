Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common motorcycle accessory questions

For Indian roads, a full-face helmet from brands like SMK, Steelbird, or Axor is recommended. Look for ISI and DOT certification, proper ventilation, and a comfortable fit. Budget between Rs 3,000 to Rs 8,000 for a good helmet. Our best helmets guide covers options for every budget.

The best engine oil depends on your motorcycle type and usage. Motul 7100 10W-50 is excellent for performance bikes, while Motul 3100 10W-40 suits commuter motorcycles. Always check your owner's manual for the recommended grade. See our best engine oil guide for detailed recommendations.

Lubricate your chain every 500 km for city riding, or every 300 km during highway tours. Use a quality chain lube and always clean the chain before applying fresh lubricant. Check our best chain lubes for top picks.

The best phone mounts are vibration-free models from Bobo, Givi, or SP Connect. They protect your phone's camera from vibration damage while providing a secure hold on handlebars. Our best phone mounts guide reviews all top options.

Yes, a waterproof bike cover protects against sun, rain, dust, and bird droppings. Look for covers with UV-resistant coating and proper ventilation to prevent moisture buildup. See our best bike covers for recommendations.

A portable digital tyre inflator is essential for emergency repairs. Look for models with digital gauges, LED lights, and 12V power compatibility. Inflate your tyres to the recommended PSI (typically 28-32 for rear, 22-25 for front). Our best tyre inflators guide has you covered.

Your Complete Guide to Motorcycle Accessories and Gear in India

Finding the right motorcycle accessories in India can be overwhelming with hundreds of options across dozens of categories. Whether you are a daily commuter navigating city traffic or a touring enthusiast planning your next Himalayan adventure, having the right gear makes every ride safer and more enjoyable.

At BikeReview India, we understand that every motorcycle has different needs. A Royal Enfield Classic 350 requires different accessories than a Yamaha MT-15 or a KTM Duke 390. That is why our buying guides are motorcycle-specific, recommending products that actually fit and work with your particular model.

When it comes to riding gear, safety should never be compromised. A quality helmet is the single most important purchase any rider can make. We test and review helmets across price ranges from budget-friendly options under Rs 3,000 to premium international brands. Our guides cover full-face, modular, and open-face options suited for Indian weather conditions.

Motorcycle maintenance is another area where the right products make a significant difference. Using the correct engine oil grade, maintaining your chain with quality chain lube, and protecting your bike with a good bike cover can dramatically extend your motorcycle's lifespan. Our maintenance guides break down exactly what you need and when you need it.

For riders who rely on navigation, a vibration-free phone mount is essential. Modern phone mounts protect your smartphone's camera from vibration damage while keeping your GPS visible and accessible. We have tested dozens of options to find the most reliable mounts for Indian road conditions.

Whether you are shopping for riding gloves for monsoon commutes, a crash guard for off-road adventures, or a tyre inflator for long-distance touring, our expert recommendations help you make informed decisions. Every product we recommend has been researched thoroughly, with honest pros and cons so you know exactly what to expect.
