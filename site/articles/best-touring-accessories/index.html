Best Touring Accessories for Long Distance Riding

Long-distance motorcycle touring is one of the greatest joys of riding. Whether you're heading to the Himalayas, exploring coastal roads, or crossing states on your Royal Enfield, the right accessories transform a good ride into a great one. This guide covers the essential touring accessories that make long-distance riding comfortable, safe, and enjoyable.

Why Touring Accessories Matter

Comfort

Long hours in the saddle take a toll on your body. Quality touring accessories reduce fatigue, improve ergonomics, and let you ride longer with less strain.

Storage

Touring requires carrying gear, clothing, tools, and supplies. Proper luggage systems keep everything organized and secure without affecting the motorcycle's handling.

Safety

Additional lighting, visibility aids, and protective equipment become more important when riding in unfamiliar areas and varying conditions.

Convenience

Small additions like USB chargers, tank pads, and phone mounts make the journey smoother and more enjoyable.

Essential Touring Accessories

Tank Bags

Tank bags sit on top of your fuel tank, providing easy access to essentials while riding.

Benefits: - Quick access to phone, wallet, and documents - Maps and navigation visible at a glance - Small items stay organized - Doesn't affect motorcycle handling

What to look for: - Magnetic or ring-lock mounting system - Rain cover included - Multiple compartments - Clear map/phone pocket - Compatible with your tank shape

Capacity guide: | Size | Capacity | Best For | |------|----------|----------| | Small | 5-10L | Day trips, essentials only | | Medium | 10-15L | Weekend tours | | Large | 15-25L | Multi-day tours |

Saddle Bags

Saddle bags mount on either side of the rear seat or subframe, carrying heavier items low and centered.

Benefits: - Large storage capacity - Low center of gravity for better handling - Even weight distribution - Secure mounting

What to look for: - Waterproof construction or included rain covers - Quick-release mounting system - Reflective elements for visibility - Reinforced bottoms for durability - Lockable zippers

Types: - Soft saddle bags: Lighter, more flexible, easier to store when not in use - Hard saddle bags: More protection, lockable, weather-resistant - Throw-over: Quick to install, no permanent mounting required

Tail Bags

Mounted on the rear seat or rack, tail bags offer additional storage without affecting side clearance.

Best for: - Supplementing saddle bags - Carrying items needed during the day - Motorcycles without pillion seats - Light touring setups

Windscreen

A windscreen deflects wind over the rider, reducing fatigue and improving comfort at highway speeds.

Benefits: - Reduces wind blast on chest and helmet - Less rider fatigue on long highway stretches - Better weather protection - Improved aerodynamics

Types: | Type | Height | Best For | |------|--------|----------| | Short | Below chin | City/highway mix | | Medium | Chin level | All-round touring | | Tall | Above head | Maximum wind protection |

What to look for: - Model-specific fit - Adjustable height (if possible) - Clear or lightly tinted - Scratch-resistant coating - Vibration dampening

USB Charger

Keep your devices charged during long rides. Essential for navigation and communication.

What to look for: - Waterproof construction - Multiple ports (USB-A and USB-C) - Voltage regulation (protects devices) - Easy installation - On/off switch to prevent battery drain

Installation tips: - Connect directly to battery with inline fuse - Route cables away from moving parts - Use cable ties to secure wiring - Ensure waterproof connection

Comfort Accessories

Gel Seat Pad

Reduces pressure points and improves comfort during long hours in the saddle.

Benefits: - Distributes weight evenly - Reduces numbness and pressure points - Absorbs road vibrations - Easy to install and remove

Types: - Gel pads: Maximum comfort, heavier - Air pads: Lightweight, adjustable firmness - Memory foam: Good comfort, contours to body

Handlebar Risers

Raise the handlebars to reduce back and shoulder strain on touring motorcycles.

Best for: - Riders experiencing back pain - Changing from sport to touring ergonomics - Long-distance comfort improvement

Foot Pegs

Wider, rubber-dampened footpegs reduce vibration and fatigue in feet and legs.

What to look for: - Rubber dampening for vibration isolation - Wide platform for comfort - Anti-slip surface - Compatible with your motorcycle

Visibility and Safety

Auxiliary Lights

Additional lighting improves visibility to other road users and helps you see better at night.

Types: - LED fog lights: Low power consumption, excellent visibility - Spot lights: Long-range illumination for highway riding - DRL strips: Daytime running lights for visibility

Installation tips: - Mount at appropriate height to avoid blinding oncoming traffic - Use a relay for proper power management - Consider a separate switch for auxiliary lights - Ensure legal compliance in your state

Reflective Tape

Increases visibility at night with minimal investment. Apply to: - Helmet - Luggage - Motorcycle frame - Jacket and pants

First Aid Kit

Essential for any tour, especially in remote areas where medical help may be far away.

Include: - Bandages and gauze - Antiseptic wipes - Pain relievers - Anti-diarrheal medication - Insect repellent - Emergency contact information - Basic medical guide

Maintenance on Tour

Portable Tool Kit

Carry essential tools for roadside repairs and adjustments:

  • Allen key set
  • Screwdrivers
  • Pliers
  • Tire repair kit
  • Portable inflator
  • Spare fuses
  • Chain adjustment tool
  • Cable ties and electrical tape

Chain Maintenance Kit

Keep your chain in good condition during long tours:

  • Small chain lube spray
  • Chain cleaning brush
  • Clean rag

Luggage Configuration Guide

Day Trip

  • Tank bag (5-10L)
  • Backpack for extra items

Weekend Tour (1-2 Days)

  • Tank bag (10-15L)
  • Tail bag (15-20L)

Extended Tour (3+ Days)

  • Tank bag (10-15L)
  • Saddle bags (30-40L total)
  • Tail bag (15-20L)

Adventure Touring

  • Tank bag (15-20L)
  • Hard saddle bags (40-50L total)
  • Rear rack with duffel (30-40L)

Packing Tips

  1. Balance weight: Distribute weight evenly between saddle bags
  2. Heavy items low: Keep heavy items in saddle bags, not tank bag
  3. Essentials accessible: Phone, wallet, documents in tank bag
  4. Waterproof everything: Use dry bags inside soft luggage
  5. Minimize: Pack light — you can always buy what you forget
  6. Secure loose items: Use bungee cords or net for overflow

Conclusion

The right touring accessories transform your motorcycle from a daily commuter into a long-distance machine. Start with the essentials — luggage system, windscreen, and USB charger — and add more as you identify your needs on each tour. Remember, the best touring setup is one that suits your riding style, motorcycle, and the type of touring you do. Invest in quality, pack light, and enjoy the journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

Comfort is king on long rides. A good seat or gel pad, proper luggage system, and windscreen make the biggest difference in rider comfort over hundreds of kilometers.

A general rule is to keep total luggage weight under 15-20% of the motorcycle's curb weight. For most Indian motorcycles, this means 15-25 kg total including rider and pillion.

Both serve different purposes. Tank bags are great for frequently accessed items like phones and wallets. Saddle bags carry more cargo and keep weight lower. Most touring riders use both.

A windscreen significantly reduces wind blast and rider fatigue on highways. For touring distances over 200 km, a windscreen is highly recommended.
